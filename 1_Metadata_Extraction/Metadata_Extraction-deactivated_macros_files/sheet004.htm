new_tests_smoothed According to OWID, for the countries that did not report new tests on a daily basis, to produce a complete series of daily figures for calculating the 7-day rolling average, the assumption was made that testing changed equally on a daily basis over the periods on which no data was reported. Contrary to this statement, there are at least 2 known instances in which this does not concur. A) Afganistan, year 2022: There were no counts listed in the entirety of the new_tests feature, only blanks; the new_tests_smoothed feature has different sets of repeating values that are consecutive, i.e., in spite of there being no new_tests counts to the respective new_tests_smoothed values, in fact, to reiterate, there were no counts for the entire feature of new_tests, there are records for Afganistan reaching from 2022-02-05 to 2022-06-23 for which values exist for the new_tests_smoothed feature. There are no counts listed in the entirety of the new_tests feature to be "changed equally on a daily basis over the periods on which no data was reported". B) Albania of Europe: The new_tests feature has consecutive blanks on the dates of 2020-03-23 and 2020-03-24 but values do exist in the respective fields of new_tests_smoothed, and hence, presumably using the surrounding data of the blanks, the data was "changed equally on a daily basis over the periods on which no data was reported". With that being said, these values of the new_tests feature are not listed since they were not recorded, i.e., they are still blanks. The hypothetical counts required in place of these blanks to create the existing respective values in the new_tests_smoothed feature would be around the ballpark of 40 and 34, consecutively. By trial and error, ~ 40 is the hypothetical count that would allow for its respective new_tests_smoothed value to coexist. Once the hypothetical 40 is in place, the same is true of the hypothetical 34. However, the problem lies in how 40 and 34 are the hypothetical values that exist due to its' previous counts being "changed equally on a daily basis over the periods on which no data was reported", i.e., the counts 40 and 34 cannot concur with OWID's statement. This is because the previous tangible change was a -28% drop, then to reach the hypothetical 40 there was a +21% increase, and then to reach the hypothetical 34 there was a -15% drop. Hence, these percents of change being unequal, the hypothetical counts don't appear to be as a result of being "changed equally on a daily basis over the periods on which no data was reported". Of the preceding counts which are also tangible, there is still too much fluctuation in change between those counts for the hypothetical counts to exist due to being "changed equally on a daily basis over the periods on which no data was reported". 
new_vaccinations_smoothed According to OWID, for the countries that did not report new tests on a daily basis, to produce a complete series of daily figures for calculating the 7-day rolling average, the assumption was made that testing changed equally on a daily basis over the periods on which no data was reported. However, this may not be the case as it was not in the new_tests_smoothed feature.
new_people_vaccinated_smoothed The dataset did not include this feature's base feature, i.e., new_people_vaccinated. Presumably, as per its title, this feature represents people receiving their first dose but not their second, third, and/or final dose. Hence, as per its title, the values of this feature may be a subset of the new_vaccinations feature. Upon comparing the 7-day smoothed values of both features, it was confirmed that approximately 95% of the comparisons made were coherrent, i.e., the number of people who received their first vaccine dose on a particular date (new_people_vaccinated) was less than or equal to the number of new vaccinations that took place on that same date (new_vaccinations). The remaining 5% was comprised of 7,218 anomalous records/dates in which the number of new people who received their first vaccination was greater than the number of new vaccinations. This anomalous proportion may be attributed to both features being 7-day smoothed values.
excess_mortality According to OWID's data explicitly pertaining to excess mortality, changes were implemented on Sept 20 2021 and Jan 18 2022 in calculating the p-score. 
excess_mortality_cumulative Similar to the excess_mortality feature, changes may have been implemented in calculating the p-score in this instance as well, however, the operands were also cumulative in this instance. A further comparitive analysis would be necessary on the features of the excess_mortality.csv file to confirm this.
reproduction_rate According to OWID's repo, more information can be found on this feature at this link: https://github.com/crondonm/TrackingR/tree/main/Estimates-Database.
stringency_index At certain locations policies may vary by sub-region due to some sub-regions being more concentrated than others with COVID-19, in which case, the most strictest sub-region's stringency index will represent the entire location. These government policies may also differ by vaccination status, and hence, a stringency index was calculated at each location for 3 categories, which are vaccinated, non-vaccinated, and national average based on vaccinated and non-vaccinated.
