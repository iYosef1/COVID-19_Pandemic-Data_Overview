Dataset Features Data Type Feature Description
continent Categorical; nominal Continent is the geograhical region of which the country  belongs to, e.g., Canada belongs to the continent of North America.
location Categorical; nominal Location in our dataset is the country matching with its respective ISO code.
date Numerical; discrete This is the date (YYYY-MM-DD) on which the data was observed and recorded. In our dataset, there is apparently a daily record, i.e., a report was submitted per day. Contextually, the date being discrete, and hence quantifiable, it also behaves as a categorical feature, i.e., it can be grouped into weeks, months, quarters, and years.
iso_code Categorical; nominal ISO 3166-1 is a set of codes used to uniquely each represent  country. There are 3 subversions within ISO 3166-1 and the version used in our data is the Alpha-3 code, e.g., CAN is the code representing Canada. In this dataset, there are also instances of OWID-defined regions, e.g., continents such as "Europe" will contain the prefix "OWID_".
population Numerical; discrete This is the number of people living within a location or country. The population count was not updated according to the death counts throughout the pandemic. The population count may also vary depending on the source from which it was collected from. The locations' populations have likely been used as a constant within the calculations of other existing features in our dataset.

This is a fixed feature value for each location.
population_density Numerical; continuous This number represents the number of people (or population) divided by the land area of the location measured in the unit of square kilometers, e.g., 10 people per square km or 10/km². This is a fixed feature value for each location.
median_age Numerical; continuous This is the median age of the population of each location. This is a fixed feature value for each location.
aged_65_older Numerical; continuous This is the share of population that is 65 years and older. The values have been apparently provided as percentages. This is a fixed feature value for each location.
aged_70_older Numerical; continuous This is the share of population that is 70 years and older. The values have been apparently provided as percentages. This is a fixed feature value for each location.
gdp_per_capita Numerical; continuous This number is the inflation-adjusted GDP (Gross Domestic Product) at PPP (Purchasing Power Parity) divided by the population of a location to acquire a value that allows for comparisons to be made between locations at the person-level and purchasing power of a location. Each value in this feature is the economical output per person in a particular year at a location which has been eliminated of currency exchange rate and represents the true average productivity of a location, viable for comparison globally. GDP in itself is the standard of living within a country or location in a year, or in other words, it is the total value of goods and services purchased by the people of a location in a year. PPP is a metric that "normalizes" currency exchange rates into international dollars, so to speak, by directing GDP into a "basket of goods". In other words, PPP adjusts the different locations' GDP to its respective purchasing power of similar/same goods and services from different locations so that the effects of different currency exchange rates have been eliminated. Inflation being the rate at which the general level of prices for goods and services is rising, GDP can be adjusted for inflation by being made "constant" in order to reflect the true PPP of the GDP without the distortion caused by inflation.

This is a fixed feature value for each location.
extreme_poverty Numerical; continuous This is the share of population living in extreme poverty. The values have been apparently provided as percentages. This is a fixed feature value for each location.
cardiovasc_death_rate Numerical; continuous  This number is the death rate from cardiovascular disease, i.e., the annual number of deaths per 100,000 people. This is a fixed feature value for each location.
diabetes_prevalence Numerical; continuous This is the percentage of a population aging from 20 to 79 that is representative of diabetes prevalence. This is a fixed feature value for each location.
female_smokers Numerical; continuous This is the share of women in a population who smoke. The values have been apparently provided as percentages. This is a fixed feature value for each location.
male_smokers Numerical; continuous This is the share of men in a population who smoke. The values have been apparently provided as percentages. This is a fixed feature value for each location.
handwashing_facilities Numerical; continuous This is the share of population with basic handwashing facilities on premises. The values have been apparently provided as percentages. This is a fixed feature value for each location.
hospital_beds_per_thousand Numerical; continuous This is the number of hospital beds per 1,000 people. This is a fixed feature value for each location.
life_expectancy Numerical; continuous Also known as "life expectancy at birth", this is the average number of years a person is expected to live. It is indicative of a population's health. Asides from COVID-19, note that this feature is bound to be affected by other features such as GDP. This is a fixed feature value for each location.
human_development_index Numerical; continuous The HDI (human development index) is a summary measure that represents the quality of life in a country/location. This measure is on a scale from 0 to 1 with 1 indicating a very high quality of life. It is determined by 3 dimensions:

1) Health index: This dimension is represented by the life expectancy at birth.
2) Education index: This dimension is represented by the mean of years of schooling for adults and expected years of schooling for children of school-entering age.
3) Income index: This dimension is represented by GNI (Gross National Income) per capita. GNI is a location's true monetary value, i.e., it is the GDP plus the incomes earned by residents overseas, minus the incomes earned by non-residents in the domestic economy. The GNI per capita is the GNI divided by the location's population.

This is a fixed feature value for each location.
new_tests Numerical; discrete This is the number of new COVID tests conducted each day.
new_tests_per_thousand Numerical; continuous This is the number of new COVID tests conducted each day per proportion size of 1,000.
new_tests_smoothed Numerical; continuous This number denotes the 7-day rolling average of new tests.
new_tests_smoothed_per_thousand Numerical; continuous This number denotes the 7-day rolling average of new tests per thousand. It can be obtained by applying the appropriate calculation to either feature, i.e., the new_tests_per_thousand or the new_tests_smoothed.
total_tests Numerical; discrete This is the total cumulative COVID tests conducted each day from the first day.
total_tests_per_thousand Numerical; continuous This is the total cumulative COVID tests conducted each day from the first day per proportion size of 1,000.
positive_rate
Numerical; continuous This field represents the proportion of COVID tests that came back positive, provided as a rolling 7-day average. The most probable equation used to obtain the values of this feature would be as follows: positive_rate = new_cases_smoothed / new_tests_smoothed. As per the analysis conducted, there is some variance between the equation's values which were calculated and its respective provided positive_rate values, however, the frequency of this variance is exponentially declining as the variance itself gets larger. Also, there are many locations (as a whole or partially by dates) at which the positive_rate fields could not be validated via comparison with the calculated feature's fields, and vice versa.
tests_per_case Numerical; continuous This field represents the number of tests conducted per new confirmed case of COVID, provided as a 7-day rolling average. It is the inverse of the positive_rate feature, i.e., it is tests_per_case = 1 / positive_rate, which is unsurprisingly more accurate than the values provided by the inversed calculation (new_tests_smoothed / new_cases_smoothed). The analysis mentioned in the description of the positive_rate feature was applied to this feature and the results were essentially identical.
tests_units Categorical; nominal This is the reported unit by which COVID testing was conducted. A country/location cannot contain mixed units. All metrics related to testing will have used the same testing unit. The 4 different test units of this feature are the following:

a) People Tested: The number of people tested.
b) Tests Performed: The number of tests performed, i.e., a single person could have been tested more than once in a given day.
c) Samples Tested: The number of samples tested, i.e., there cases in which more than one sample may be required to perform a given test.
new_cases Numerical; discrete This is the number of new cases of covid that occurred each day. Predominantly, this value will either consistently increase or decrease but  it can also fluctuate randomly. As with the total_cases feature, new_cases will be likely to consist of false positives and incorrect diagnoses. This granular count can be aggregated for the new cases that occurred within a particular week, month, and/or year. The counts in this field can include probable cases where recorded. 
new_cases_per_million Numerical; continuous This is the number of new Covid cases that occurred each day per proportion size of 1,000,000.
new_cases_smoothed Numerical; continuous This number denotes the 7-day rolling average of new cases.
new_cases_smoothed_per_million Numerical; continuous This number denotes the 7-day rolling average of new cases per million.
total_cases Numerical; discrete This is the total cumulative number of Covid-19 cases each day from the first day. This number should typically be increasing from where it starts at 1 since it represents total count till date. In a case in which the subsequent total count has dropped from the previous, this may be due to a reason such as an incorrect diagnosis or false positive. This may also have been a common occurance at the early stages of the corona outbreak when the best tools were not available to test for covid. At the time of recording, the counts in this field could also have included probable cases.
total_cases_per_million Numerical; continuous This total cumulative number of Covid-19 cases occuring each day per proportion size of 1,000,000.
hosp_patients
Numerical; discrete This field exclusively represents the number of patients on a date who were diagnosed with COVID-19 and had to be hospitalized, i.e., it is the present head count of patients with COVID in the hospital and is not inclusive of those in the ICU due to COVID. A field in this feature can be inclusive of existing COVID-free patients who later contracted COVID or patients who were once in the ICU but had improvements in their condition. Note that a single day or constituent of a single 7-day summation which collectively comprises the weekly_hosp_admissions feature is a subset of this feature's respective record as it represents new hospital admissions.
hosp_patients_per_million Numerical; continuous This is the number of hospital patients each day per proportion size of 1,000,000.
icu_patients Numerical; discrete This field exclusively represents those who have been admitted into the Intensive Care Unit (ICU) due to COVID-19. This may include existing hospital patients who were transferred or altogether new patients.
icu_patients_per_million Numerical; continuous This is the number of ICU patients each day per proportion size of 1,000,000.
weekly_hosp_admissions Numerical; discrete This field represents the weekly number of new COVID-19 patients admitted to hospitals. It is the summation of new admissions on a date plus the new admissions of the preceding 6 days. This count may be inclusive of patients who were once in the ICU but are no longer in need of any intensive care. This feature is mutually exclusive from the weekly_icu_admissions feature.
weekly_hosp_admissions_per_million Numerical; continuous This is the number of weekly admissions of new hospital patients each day per proportion size of 1,000,000.
weekly_icu_admissions Numerical; discrete This field represents the weekly number of new COVID-19 patients admitted to the ICU. It is the summation of new admissions on a date plus the new admissions of the preceding 6 days. This may include existing hospital patients or new patients altogether.
weekly_icu_admissions_per_million Numerical; continuous This is the number of weekly admissions of new ICU patients each day per proportion size of 1,000,000.
new_deaths Numerical; discrete This is the number of new deaths that occurred each day. This is typically a confirmed value that is unlikely to be erred, however, there may be instances in which death was the most probable outcome for a patient and was recorded as such.
new_deaths_per_million Numerical; continuous This is the number of new deaths that occurred each day per proportion size of 1,000,000.
new_deaths_smoothed Numerical; continuous This number denotes the 7-day rolling average of new deaths.
new_deaths_smoothed_per_million Numerical; continuous This number denotes the 7-day rolling average of new deaths per million.
total_deaths Numerical; discrete This is the total cumulated number of deaths that occurred each day from the first day. This is typically a confirmed value that is unlikely to be erred, however, there may be instances in which death being the most probable outcome for a patient was still recorded as an actual death. Every consecutive number within a region should either be equal to or greater than the former death count.
total_deaths_per_million Numerical; continuous This is the total cumulated number of deaths that occurred each day from the first day per proportion size of 1,000,000.
new_vaccinations
Numerical; discrete This is the number of new COVID vaccination doses administered each day. Supposedly this metric is "only calculated for consecutive days", however, there is bound to be interruption by missing fields throughout this feature. It is equally the sum of new people vaccinated (at least once), new people fully vaccinated, and new booster doses administered on any particular date because 1) total_vaccinations = people_vaccinated + people_fully_vaccinated + total_boosters, and 2) new_vaccinations' values are cummulated to create the values of the total_vaccinations feature.
new_vaccinations_smoothed Numerical; discrete This number denotes the 7-day rolling average of new vaccinations.
new_vaccinations_smoothed_per_million Numerical; discrete This number denotes the 7-day rolling average of new vaccinations per million.
people_vaccinated Numerical; discrete This is the total (cummulative) number of people who received at least one vaccine dose. This number is not inclusive of people who were fully vaccinated and/or people who received booster doses because total_vaccinations = people_vaccinated + people_fully_vaccinated + total_boosters. Certain vaccines such as Cuba's Abdala vaccine requires 3 doses by protocol, and hence, the second administration of this vaccine would be included in this count but not the third/last. Because of this equation, people_vaccinated is also a cummulative feature like total_vaccinations is.
people_vaccinated_per_hundred Numerical; continuous This is the total cummulative number of people who received at least one vaccine dose each day from the first day per proportion size of 100. 
people_fully_vaccinated Numerical; discrete This is the total (cummulative) number of people who (finally) received all doses prescribed by the initial vaccination protocol. This feature is a cummulative feature like the total_vaccinations feature because of the equation, total_vaccinations = people_vaccinated + people_fully_vaccinated + total_boosters, and hence, each subsequent value in this feature should be equal to or greater than the value before it.
people_fully_vaccinated_per_hundred Numerical; continuous This is the total cummulative number of people who (finally) received all doses (prescribed by the initial vaccination protocol) each day from the first day per proportion size of 100.
total_boosters Numerical; discrete This is the total (cummulative) number of vaccination booster doses administered beyond the number prescribed by the vaccination protocol. This feature is a cummulative feature like the total_vaccinations feature because of the equation, total_vaccinations = people_vaccinated + people_fully_vaccinated + total_boosters, and hence, each subsequent value in this feature should be equal to or greater than the value before it.
total_boosters_per_hundred Numerical; continuous This is the total cummulative number of vaccination booster doses administered (beyond the number prescribed by the vaccination protocol) each day from the first day per proportion size of 100.
total_vaccinations Numerical; discrete This is the total cummulative COVID vaccination doses administered each day from the first day, in other words, it is the total cummulated number of new vaccinations (from the new_vaccinations feature) administered each day from the first day. It is also the sum of 3 vaccination features: people_vaccinated, people_fully_vaccinated, and total_boosters. 
total_vaccinations_per_hundred Numerical; continuous This is the total cumulative COVID vaccination doses administered each day from the first day per proportion size of 100.
new_people_vaccinated_smoothed Numerical; discrete This number denotes the 7-day rolling average of the daily number of people receiving their first vaccine dose.

This is an anomalous feature due to the fact that its base feature, new_people_vaccinated, was not included in the dataset.
new_people_vaccinated_smoothed_per_hundred Numerical; continuous This number denotes the 7-day rolling average of the daily number of people receiving their first vaccine dose per hundred.
excess_mortality
Numerical; continuous This field represents the p-score which is the percentage difference between the reported number of (weekly or monthly) deaths and the projected number of deaths from previous years for the same period. The calculation for this field is as follows:  p-score = ((reported_deaths - projected_deaths) / projected_deaths) * 100. If the p-score was 100% in any given period, week or month, then that would mean the death count of that period was double or a 100% higher than the predicted/projected death count of that week. This very feature is essentially the p_proj_all_ages feature from the excess_mortality.csv file. For more details, the excess_mortality_metadata.pdf file can be referred to.
excess_mortality_cumulative Numerical; continuous The p-score being represented in this field is the percentage difference between the cumulative number of deaths since Jan 01 2020 and the cumulative projected deaths for the same period based on previous years. The calculation for this field is identical to that of the excess_mortality feature except that the operands are cumulative, i.e., this feature is not calculatable from the excess_mortality feature, and hence, it will be treated as a base feature. This feature is essentially the cum_p_proj_all_ages feature from the excess_mortality.csv file. For more details, the excess_mortality_metadata.pdf file can be referred to.
excess_mortality_cumulative_absolute Numerical; continuous This field is the cumulative number of excess deaths, or in other words, the cumulative difference between the reported number of deaths since Jan 01 2020 and the projected number of deaths for the same period based on previous years. The calculation for this field is as follows: excess_deaths = reported_deaths - projected_deaths, with the operands or the final result being cumulated from Jan 01 2020. This feature is essentially the cum_excess_proj_all_ages feature from the excess_mortality.csv file. For more details, the excess_mortality_metadata.pdf file can be referred to.

This is an anomalous feature due to the fact that its base feature, excess_mortality_absolute, was not included in the dataset.
excess_mortality_cumulative_per_million Numerical; continuous This field is the excess_mortality_cumulative_absolute feature's value (cumulative number of excess deaths) per proportion size of 1,000,000. This feature can be calculated from the excess_mortality_cumulative_absolute feature, however, there will be noteworthy variances observed between the provided and calculated features. Either versions may be ideal for analysis.
reproduction_rate Numerical; continuous This number is the real-time estimate of the effective reproduction rate. It is also referred to as R naught (R0) and it measures how fast a virus is spreading, particularly as the average number of individuals who will contract the virus from a single infected person in a population with no immunity. It's calculations is reliant upon 3 factors, 1) the contact rate, 2) the risk or ease of infection upon contact, and 3) the length of time an infected person is contagious. When R0 = 1, each infected person on average will infect one other person and the population will essentially remain unchanged. When R0 > 1, the disease is spreading, and when R0 < 1, the virus is declining. Respectively of the latter statement, the larger R0 is from 1, the faster the virus is spreading, and the smaller it is from 1, the more the virus has been contained and is now declining. This number is used by public health officials to understand the spread of a virus in order to determine the measures necessary to deal with the spread.
stringency_index Numerical; continuous Also known as the Government Response Stringency Index, it is essentially the strictness of government policies. The stringency index is a calculation made on any given date by the Oxford Coronavirus Government Response Tracker (OxCGRT) project as the mean of 9 response indicators' out-of-100 scores:

1. School closures
2. Workplace closures
3. Cancellation of public events
4. Restrictions on public gatherings
5. Closures of public transport
6. Stay-at-home requirements
7. Public information campaigns
8. Restrictions on internal movements
9. International travel controls

This score is not indicative by any measure of a country's response being better than another's. A higher score simply means a stricter response due to circumstances, with 100 being the highest score. Dependent on the location, if there are variances in policies by sub-region and/or by vaccination status, 1) the index of the strictest sub-region and/or 2) the national weighted average of both vaccinated and non-vaccinated (presumably over the isolated vaccinated or non-vaccinated sets) will be used, respectively.
